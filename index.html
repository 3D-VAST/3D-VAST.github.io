Introduction

As large-scale 3D scene modeling becomes increasingly important for applications such as urban planning, robotics, autonomous navigation, and virtual simulations, the need for diverse, high-quality visual data is greater than ever. Combining images from multiple altitudes offers a promising solution to overcome traditional limitations, enabling richer, more complete 3D reconstructions.

Core Topics

Technical Challenges

  • Sparse and incomplete views
  • Visual ambiguities across altitudes
  • Spatio-temporal inconsistencies

Research Topics

  2. Neural scene representations
  3. Urban digital twins
